# Aperto Bible - Image Specifications

## Visual Philosophy

**Style:** Kinfolk/Alabaster-style editorial conceptual photography
**Approach:** Art-directed interpretations that evoke the passage's emotional and thematic core—not literal biblical illustrations

### Reference Points
- **Alabaster Co.** - Their Bible photography: intimate, conceptual, modern
- **Kinfolk Magazine** - Muted tones, intentional negative space, human moments
- **Cereal Magazine** - Quiet contemplation, architectural simplicity

### Technical Style
- **Film aesthetic**: Analog grain, slightly desaturated
- **Color palette**: Muted earth tones, soft light, cream/beige/sage
- **Mood**: Contemplative, intimate, unhurried
- **Composition**: Editorial negative space, room to breathe
- **Human subjects**: Diverse, contemporary, real—not "biblical costumes"
- **No literal depictions**: Abstract, emotional, conceptual interpretations

## Luke 1 - Pericope Images

### 1. Prologue (1:1-4)
**Theme:** Careful investigation, eyewitness testimony, writing as devotion

**Concept:** Hands writing in a notebook or journal. Natural light from a window. Stack of well-worn books nearby. Focus on the act of careful, intentional documentation.

**Art direction:**
- Close-up of hands with pen on paper
- Warm morning light, soft shadows
- Analog film grain
- Cream/warm white paper, dark ink

**Emotional core:** Dedication, care, intentionality

---

### 2. An Old Priest, an Empty Nursery (1:5-25)
**Theme:** Faithful waiting, unanswered prayer, divine interruption

**Concept:** An empty chair by a window. Or: aged hands holding something small and precious. The visual vocabulary of "waiting that has become a way of life."

**Art direction:**
- Soft, diffused afternoon light
- Muted sage/olive tones
- Single object in frame, negative space
- Texture of age: worn wood, linen, patina

**Emotional core:** Patience worn into habit, hope nearly extinguished

---

### 3. Mary Says Yes (1:26-38)
**Theme:** Radical consent, courage in uncertainty, saying yes to the unknown

**Concept:** A young woman at a threshold—doorway, window, edge of frame. Looking outward or inward. The moment before stepping forward.

**Art direction:**
- Subject at edge of frame, facing light or open space
- Soft natural light, possibly morning
- Simple clothing, contemporary
- Warm skin tones against cool background

**Emotional core:** Vulnerable courage, quiet determination

---

### 4. The Visitation (1:39-45)
**Theme:** Female solidarity, shared secret, joy in recognition

**Concept:** Two women's hands touching. Or: two figures in silhouette, leaning toward each other. Intimacy between women, not performative.

**Art direction:**
- Warm tones, golden hour light
- Focus on connection: hands, posture, proximity
- Soft background, subjects sharp
- Feeling of privacy, sacred moment

**Emotional core:** Being known, being believed, mutual joy

---

### 5. The Magnificat (1:46-56)
**Theme:** Prophetic defiance, reversal of power, God sides with the overlooked

**Concept:** A woman from below, looking up—or standing elevated, looking down. Power dynamics inverted. Could also be: hands raised, open stance, expansive gesture.

**Art direction:**
- Strong composition, unusual angle
- Subject takes up space, commands frame
- Possibly architectural elements suggesting scale
- Warm earth tones with one strong accent

**Emotional core:** Defiance, exultation, prophetic authority

---

### 6. The Birth of John (1:57-66)
**Theme:** Community witness, naming as identity, speech restored

**Concept:** Hands writing on a tablet or paper. Or: a group gathered around a central focus we can't quite see—backs of heads, shoulders, attention directed inward.

**Art direction:**
- Warm, celebratory light
- Multiple figures but not crowded
- Focus on gesture: writing, pointing, leaning in
- Communal without being chaotic

**Emotional core:** Witness, recognition, restoration

---

### 7. The Benedictus (1:67-79)
**Theme:** Prophetic vision, dawn breaking, covenant faithfulness

**Concept:** First light on a landscape. Or: a figure facing sunrise, silhouetted. The vocabulary of "dawn from on high."

**Art direction:**
- Horizontal orientation, landscape
- Golden/pink dawn light
- Silhouette or partial figure
- Expansive negative space (sky)

**Emotional core:** Hope returning, darkness receding

---

### 8. Wilderness Years (1:80)
**Theme:** Hidden growth, desert formation, waiting for readiness

**Concept:** An empty landscape with minimal human trace. Path, horizon, vast sky. Or: a single figure very small in frame, suggesting solitude and formation.

**Art direction:**
- Desaturated earth tones, minimal palette
- Vast negative space
- Human figure small or absent
- Texture: sand, rock, dried grass

**Emotional core:** Solitude, preparation, hiddenness

## Production Notes

### For AI Image Generation (Midjourney/DALL-E)
- Include "Kinfolk magazine editorial photography style"
- Include "analog film grain, Kodak Portra 400"
- Include "muted earth tones, soft natural light"
- Include "conceptual, not literal biblical illustration"
- Avoid: halos, robes, obvious religious imagery

### For Photo Shoots
- Natural locations: homes, gardens, simple architecture
- Diverse casting: contemporary, not "biblical"
- Wardrobe: simple, earth tones, natural fabrics
- Time of day: golden hour, soft morning light

### Localization
Each image should work for both English and German contexts. Avoid text in images. If hands are writing, keep text illegible/abstract or use both languages in variants.

Examples:

luke_01_prologue_de.jpg

For language-neutral images (no text, universal concepts):
```

```
